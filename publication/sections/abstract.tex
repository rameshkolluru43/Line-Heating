\begin{abstract}
Line heating is widely used in shipbuilding to induce controlled curvature in steel plates. This paper presents a 3D coupled thermo-mechanical finite element workflow for ship-plate line heating that supports (i) forward simulation---given heating patterns, predict temperature and deformation---and (ii) inverse planning---given target curvature, optimize heating lines. We describe the governing equations (transient heat conduction with a moving Gaussian heat source, convection and optional radiation; linear thermoelasticity with an optional inherent-strain surrogate for residual bending), the numerical discretization (linear tetrahedral elements, backward Euler in time), and the software pipeline. The workflow is validated against four published experimental benchmark cases from Li et al.\ (2023), with mean absolute percentage error in midspan residual deflection of \SI{0.52}{\%} and maximum \SI{1.77}{\%}. We demonstrate application to a representative keel-plate section with multiple heating lines and report deflection and curvature metrics. All inputs, outputs, and scripts are available in the project repository for reproducibility.
\end{abstract}
