\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

A 3D coupled thermo-mechanical finite element workflow for ship-plate line heating has been presented and validated against Li et al.\ \cite{li2023} Q345 benchmark data. The workflow includes transient heat conduction with moving Gaussian and induction-style line heat sources, temperature-dependent Q345 properties (Table~1), convection, quenching, optional radiation, linear thermoelasticity, and a calibrated inherent-strain surrogate for residual bending.

Validation on eleven published Table~2 experiments (cases 1--7, 87--90) yields MAPE \SI{1.17}{\%} in midspan deflection (maximum \SI{2.78}{\%}) for the moving-torch baseline; the four-case calibration subset achieves MAPE \SI{0.52}{\%}, while the seven non-anchor validation cases achieve MAPE \SI{1.55}{\%}. Multi-pass and high-energy conditions are predicted within \SI{0.8}{\%}. A velocity-dependent post-calibration using Cases~1--7 reduces the speed-sweep MAPE from \SI{1.67}{\%} to \SI{0.71}{\%}, showing that the speed trend can be corrected with a small exponent in the inherent-strain law. The induction-style study demonstrates that pass scheduling is essential: a collapsed quench-enabled full-line heat underpredicts two-pass deflections by about \SI{50}{\%}, while sequential line heating with quench matches the multi-pass subset with MAPE \SI{0.27}{\%}. Keel-plate demonstrations illustrate application to multi-line industrial geometry, but are not claimed as experimental validation.

The previously identified verification items have been advanced where the archived dataset permits: the velocity-dependent inherent-strain calibration, keel-plate thermal-unit audit, mesh/energy reporting scaffold, induction skin-depth heat source, Case~6 mesh/time-step sensitivity matrix, and diagnostic Case~6 J2 elastoplastic spot check are now generated reproducibly from repository outputs. The mesh/time-step study shows that local heating-band mesh resolution dominates the Case~6 discretization sensitivity, while halving $\Delta t$ at the baseline mesh changes $|w|_{\max}$ by only \SI{0.03}{\%}. A more complete elastoplastic validation and a full Maxwell coil-field induction solver require further physics development; Table~2 rows 8--86 remain blocked until full experimental data become available. All inputs and outputs required to reproduce the completed results are archived in the accompanying project repository.
