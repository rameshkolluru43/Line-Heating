\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

A 3D coupled thermo-mechanical finite element workflow for ship-plate line heating has been presented and validated. The workflow includes transient heat conduction with a moving Gaussian heat source, convection and optional radiation, linear thermoelasticity, and an optional inherent-strain surrogate for residual bending. Validation against four experimental cases from Li et al.\ \cite{li2023} yields a mean absolute percentage error in midspan deflection of \SI{0.52}{\%} and a maximum of \SI{1.77}{\%}. Keel-plate demonstrations show consistent deflection trends with heating intensity and multi-pass scheduling.

Planned work includes: (i) normalizing thermal units across runs and re-running selected cases in consistent Kelvin; (ii) mesh and time-step convergence studies and energy-balance reporting; (iii) full-resolution sequential keel-plate runs; (iv) optional integration of an elastoplastic residual-stress model; and (v) comparison with additional experimental or shipyard reference data where available. All inputs, outputs, and scripts are provided in the project repository for reproducibility.
