\subsection{Li et al.\ (2023) Benchmark Cases}
Validation is performed against four experimental cases from Li et al.\ \cite{li2023} (midspan residual deflection). The inherent-strain surrogate was calibrated to match these conditions; the same parameters are used for all four cases. \Cref{tab:li2023} summarizes predicted vs.\ experimental deflection and peak temperature.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\caption{Li et al.\ (2023) validation: predicted vs.\ experimental midspan residual deflection (v8 calibrated runs).}
\label{tab:li2023}
\begin{tabular}{rrrrrrrrr}
\toprule
Case & $v$ (mm/s) & $E$ (J/mm) & $t$ (mm) & $w_{\mathrm{exp}}$ (mm) & $w_{\mathrm{pred}}$ (mm) & $\Delta w$ (mm) & $\Delta w/w_{\mathrm{exp}}$ (\%) & $T_{\mathrm{peak}}$ (K) \\
\midrule
1   & 6.0  & 600  & 14 & 5.785 & 5.888 &  0.103 &  1.77 & 1173.6 \\
6   & 11.0 & 600  & 14 & 7.712 & 7.722 &  0.010 &  0.13 & 1176.9 \\
87  & 12.0 & 1000 & 10 & 13.506 & 13.486 & -0.020 & -0.15 & 1388.5 \\
90  & 15.0 & 1000 & 10 & 11.322 & 11.319 & -0.003 & -0.03 & 1376.5 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Mean absolute error in deflection is \SI{0.034}{mm}; mean absolute percentage error is \SI{0.52}{\%}; maximum absolute percentage error is \SI{1.77}{\%} (Case~1). \Cref{fig:li2023-t,fig:li2023-w} show representative temperature and deflection fields for Case~90.
